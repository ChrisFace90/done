What is anxiety?

Anxiety is characterized by a persistent and excessive worry about a number of different things. People with anxiety may be overly concerned about money, health, family, work, or other issues.

Anxiety comes on gradually and usually begins between childhood and middle age. There are many factors that can create anxiety, the most common ones are biological factors, family background and stressful life experiences.

Anxiety is Common

Anxiety can prevent us from doing everyday things and a lot of people experience it. Anxiety affects people all around the world.

Treatment is Possible


With treatment, people with anxiety can function socially, have full and meaningful lifes and be gainfully employed. Treating anxiety is possible with medication and therapy. As each anxiety disorder has a different set of symptoms, the types of treatment that a mental health professional may suggest also can vary.


The most common types of treatment used:


  • Psychoterapy, including cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT);

  • Medications, including anti-anxiety medications and antidepressants;

  • Complementary health approaches, including stress and relaxation techniques.

How Congregations can Help


Anxiety can make people feel lonely and hopeless. Congregations can be of a great help because:


  • A faith community can offer a vision of hope and assurance that the individual is not alone;

  • They can offer a safe, welcoming and accepting community for people to share their struggles;

  • They can offer a healthier lifestyle that reduce stress and anxiety;

  • Practices of prayer, meditation and midfulness might be helpful in creating presence and calming breathing;

  • Congregation's activities and volunteer opportunities can be healthy distractions.
